Oyakodon Recipe


Description

Oyakodon is a traditional Japanese recipe that is simple to make and delicious to taste. The name of the dish literally translates to "Mother and Child Bowl", which is a cheeky reference to the two main ingredients: chicken and eggs. Oyakodon is considered to be Japanese comfort food, and it is a favorite among children and adults.

Oyakodon belongs to a group of Japanese recipes called "donburi", which are basically rice bowls with various toppings. Other common donburi include gyuudon, a sweet and savory beef dish, and katsudon, which comes with a fried pork cutlet.

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 5 min

Cook Time: 10 min

Serves 1 person

Ingredients

  • 150g skinless boneless chicken thighs, cut in small pieces
  • 1/2 medium onion, sliced
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock or dashi
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cup steamed white short-grain rice
  • green onion, sesame oil, and nori for garnish

Instructions

  1. Combine chicken, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar in a bowl and set aside.
  2. In a small pan, bring chicken stock to a boil over medium heat. Add onion and cook about 1 minute until softened. Add the chicken mixture and cook 1-2 minutes on each side.
  3. When the chicken is almost cooked, lightly beat eggs and pour evenly Into the pan. Cook for a few seconds until the edges are nearly set.
  4. Lightly scramble the eggs and cook for a few seconds more until the eggs are slightly runny. Serve over a bowl of cooked rice, garnish, and enjoy.
