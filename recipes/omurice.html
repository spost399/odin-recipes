Omurice Recipe


Description

Omurice is an abbreviation of Omelette Rice, and it is a Japanese classic. The most basic form of the dish involves wrapping season rice inside a light, fluffy omlette. The standard form of the dish uses ketchup as the main flavor, but you can create omurice with a variety of flavors and sauces.

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 5 min

Cook Time: 10 min

Serves 1 person

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1/4 onion, diced
  • 1/4 cup carrot, diced
  • Meat and/or veggies of your choice (ham and bacon are common choices)
  • 2-3 button mushrooms, diced
  • 1 cup cooked white rice
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp milk
  • pinch of salt
  • Ketchup and parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat and add onion. Cook until fragrant. Add carrot, meat/veggies, and mushroom, and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add rice, soy sauce, and ketchup and mix to combine, ensuring all grains are evenly coated. Add to a small bowl and press down to shape the rice.
  3. Combine eggs, milk, and salt in a bowl. Heat oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat, and pour in the egg mixture. Lightly scramble for a few seconds until the eggs are about 80% cooked through. Remove from heat.
  4. Place a large plate over the bowl of rice and clip carefully to place the rice in a dome shape. Cover the rice with the egg omlette and garnish with ketchup and parsley.
