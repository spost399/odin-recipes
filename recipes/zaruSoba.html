Zaru Soba Recipe


Description

Zaru Soba is a cold noodle dish that is very popular in Japan during the summer. The noodles are served ice cold and dipped in a flavorful Mentsuyu sauce. In Japan Zaru Soba noodles are often served with a variety of toppings for people to choose from.

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 5 min

Cook Time: 5 min

Serves 2 people

Ingredients

  • 7 oz soba noodles
  • toasted sesame seeds, shredded nori, microgreens for topping
  • 1 cup dashi or chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • green onion, daikon, wasabi, or egg yolk to add to dipping sauce

Instructions

  1. Add dashi, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar to a saucepan and bring to a boil. Set aside to cool, or place in fridge/freezer to speed up the process.
  2. Cook noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse the noodles under ice-cold water, tossing them to cool evenly. Garnish noodles with nori, sesame seeds, and/or microgreens.
  3. Pour the dipping sauce into a small bowl and add desired toppings. Dip your noodles in the sauce and enjoy!
